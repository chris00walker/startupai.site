## Common Threads Across All Personas

### Universal Truths:

1. **Speed Matters More Than Perfect**
   - All three would choose "good enough in 7 days" over "perfect in 3 months"
   - Velocity enables learning; perfection delays validation

2. **Fixed Price Beats Hourly**
   - Hourly = unpredictable budget, scope creep anxiety
   - Fixed = can plan, no surprise invoices, risk transferred to vendor

3. **Deployed Product > Strategy Documents**
   - Don't want business model canvases without execution
   - "Show me the working app" beats "here's the architecture diagram"

4. **Technical Abstraction is Feature, Not Bug**
   - Don't WANT to learn tools unless they're power users (5%)
   - "You handle tech, I handle customers" is ideal division of labor

5. **Outcomes Over Processes**
   - Don't care HOW it's built (AI, contractors, your team, tools used)
   - Care about WHAT they get (URL, works, customers can use it)

### Stage 3: DECISION (Converting to Customer)

**Mental State:**
- Ready to buy IF final objections are addressed
- Looking for "reason to believe" (social proof, guarantees)
- Calculating ROI: "Is $1,500 worth it vs. alternatives?"

**Final Objections & Rebuttals:**

**Objection 1: "$1,500 is expensive for an experiment"**

❌ **Wrong Rebuttal**: "It's actually cheap compared to..."
✅ **Right Rebuttal**:
> "Let's compare your alternatives:
> - Freelancer MVP: $8-15K, 2-4 months
> - Dev shop: $25-50K, 4-6 months
> - Your time learning to code: 6-12 months opportunity cost (what's your time worth?)
>
> You're saving $6.5-48.5K and 2-12 months. The real question: What's 3 months of market learning worth to your startup? If you launch in Week 2 vs. Month 4, you gain 10 weeks of customer feedback. That's the real ROI."

**Objection 2: "What if I don't like what you build?"**

❌ **Wrong Rebuttal**: "We always deliver quality..."

> "We start with YOUR business model and get your sign-off on the scope before we build anything. Week 1 = validation + architecture approval. Week 2 = build + deploy. You review before it goes live. If it doesn't meet the scope we agreed on, we iterate at no cost (30-day quality guarantee). Plus: you own the code, so you can take it anywhere if you're not satisfied."

**Objection 3: "I need ongoing support / iterations / changes after launch"**

❌ **Wrong Rebuttal**: "That's extra..."
✅ **Right Rebuttal**:
> "Two options:
> 1. **7-Day MVP Sprint ($1,500 one-time)**: Foundation MVP + 2 iteration cycles based on early user feedback (covers Weeks 1-4)
> 2. **Founder Platform ($199/mo)**: Sprint PLUS ongoing iteration credits (100 changes/month). Most founders start with Sprint, then upgrade to Platform once they have users and need continuous improvement.
>
> Either way, you're not stuck with v1 forever."

## Key Takeaways: What Founders Actually Care About

### The Founder Doesn't Care About Your Toolchain

**They DON'T Care About:**
- Which AI model you use (Claude Code vs. Codex vs. Gemini)
- How you orchestrate tools (Bolt, Cursor, Windsurf)
- Your technical architecture decisions (microservices vs. monolith)
- Domain-driven design methodology
- Which frameworks or libraries you choose

**They DO Care About:**
- ✅ Does it work when I share the URL with customers?
- ✅ Can I get it in days/weeks, not months?
- ✅ Is the price fixed so I can budget?
- ✅ Will customers think it looks professional?
- ✅ Can I iterate when I learn what users actually want?

### The Only Three Things That Matter

**1. Velocity**
- "How fast can I get this in front of real users?"
- Speed enables learning, learning enables pivots, pivots enable success

**2. Quality**
- "Will customers think this is real or a demo?"
- Production-ready = can charge money, get real feedback, build real business

**3. Predictability**
- "Exactly what will I get, when, and for how much?"
- Removes anxiety, enables planning, transfers risk from founder to vendor

Everything else is noise.
